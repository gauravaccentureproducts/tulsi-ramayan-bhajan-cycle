SECTION 1 - PROJECT FOUNDATIONS
================================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------
1.1 VISION
------------------------------------------------------------
Compose a complete devotional bhajan cycle covering ALL SEVEN kaands of
Goswami Tulsidas's Shri Ramcharitmanas, in the AABB chaupai meter and
sonic register of Tulsidas's own Awadhi composition (as preserved in the
HMV Mukesh recordings), with each chapter framed by a Tulsidas-signature
sthayi couplet. The cycle reads as authentic Tulsidas-style verse with
selective Awadhi participles and full-syllable rhyme.

------------------------------------------------------------
1.2 SCOPE - SEVEN KAANDS
------------------------------------------------------------
   1. Bal Kand        (बाल काण्ड)        - childhood, marriage
   2. Ayodhya Kand    (अयोध्या काण्ड)     - exile, vanvas
   3. Aranya Kand     (अरण्य काण्ड)       - forest, Sita-haran
   4. Kishkindha Kand (किष्किन्धा काण्ड)   - alliance with Sugriva
   5. Sundar Kand     (सुन्दर काण्ड)       - Hanuman's Lanka journey
   6. Lanka Kand      (लंका काण्ड)         - the war
   7. Uttar Kand      (उत्तर काण्ड)        - return, Ram-rajya

Migration order: Balkand (in progress) -> Ayodhya -> Aranya -> Kishkindha
-> Sundar -> Lanka -> Uttar.

------------------------------------------------------------
1.3 FIXED PER-CHAPTER STRUCTURE (v2 - NEW)
------------------------------------------------------------
Total lines per chapter   : 31 (exact)
Chapters per kaand        : variable (chosen to preserve total episode
                             coverage; typically 6-10 per kaand)

Per-chapter anatomy:
   - Sthayi (Chorus)      : 3 lines
        Line 1+2 : Tulsidas-signature couplet (AABB internal rhyme;
                   verbatim Tulsidas where possible)
        Line 3   : Jay-ghosh refrain "सिय राम जय राम जय श्री राम॥"
                   (or context-appropriate variant)
   - 7 chaupais           : 7 x 4 padas = 28 lines
        Each chaupai      : 4 padas, AABB rhyme
                            (padas 1+2 rhyme; padas 3+4 rhyme)
        Pada length       : ~14-16 matras (Tulsidas chaupai meter)
        Rhyme strictness  : FULL-SYLLABLE TAIL must match within each
                            pair (e.g., -ीन्हा/-ीन्हा, not -ार/-हार mix)
   - Chapter total        : 3 + 28 = 31 lines

Grand total target per kaand: ~248 lines (8 chapters x 31) -- close to
the prior ~255 line target so total content per kaand is preserved.

================================================================================
SECTION 2 - THE 7-PHASE WORKFLOW
================================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 1 - PLAN
------------------------------------------------------------
1. Pick the kaand to compose.
2. Look up its canonical Tulsidas episode list (Section 3.1).
3. Distribute the episodes across the planned chapters. Each chapter
   holds 7 chaupais worth of narrative (one episode per chaupai is the
   typical pacing; closely-related episodes can share a chaupai).
4. Pick the anchor raag for this kaand (Section 3.2).
5. Pick the SthayI couplet for the kaand (Section 3.6).
6. Decide rasa modulation per chapter (Section 3.3).

------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 2 - DRAFT LYRICS (NEW v2 rules)
------------------------------------------------------------
For each chapter, write 31 lines using the Tulsidas-AABB chaupai meter.

STHAYI (3 lines):
   - Line 1+2: The kaand's signature Tulsidas couplet (Section 3.6).
     This is the recurring refrain across all chapters of the kaand
     -- the same 2 lines appear unchanged at the top of every chapter
     (and at mid-chapter chorus-returns).
   - Line 3: A jay-ghosh refrain binding the cycle:
        "सिय राम जय राम जय श्री राम॥"
     ("सिय" = Awadhi for Sita; binds with Tulsidas register.)

CHAUPAIS (28 lines, 7 chaupais x 4 padas each):
   - Each chaupai = 4 padas in AABB pattern:
        Pada 1 ............................. ।   ┐
        Pada 2 ............................. ॥   ┘ A-A pair (full-syllable rhyme)
        Pada 3 ............................. ।   ┐
        Pada 4 ............................. ॥   ┘ B-B pair (full-syllable rhyme)
   - Pada length: ~14-16 matras each.
   - Rhyme rule (Fix D): the FINAL 2 syllables must match between
     paired padas. Examples of valid pairs:
        -ीन्हा / -ीन्हा   (कीन्हा / दीन्हा)
        -ायो  / -ायो     (आयो / गयो)
        -ाई   / -ाई     (सुनाई / पाई)
        -ामा  / -ामा    (धामा / नामा)
        -ीरा  / -ीरा    (वीरा / पीरा)
   - INVALID: "-ार / -हार" mixing -- the syllable BEFORE -ार differs,
     so the tail isn't truly identical.

AWADHI PARTICIPLES (Fix C - sprinkle 2-4 per chapter for sonic flavor):
   Preferred line-end verb forms (drawn from Tulsidas):
        कीन्हा (= किया)        दीन्हा (= दिया)
        आयो (= आया)            जायो (= गया, Awadhi)
        भयो/भा (= हुआ)         गयो (= गया)
        पाई (= पाया, fem.)     सुनाई (= सुनाई)
        बढ़ाई / सजाई            दिखाई / बताई
        समाना / स्थाना          वीरा / पीरा / धीरा
        देइ (= देता/देकर)       लीन्हा (= लिया)

LANGUAGE:
   - Use a Tulsidas-flavored Hindi/Awadhi blend. The base is Awadhi
     participles at line-ends; the connective vocabulary can be modern
     Hindi or Awadhi where it scans naturally.
   - Devanagari script throughout. English permitted only in tags.
   - Avoid heavy Sanskrit padding (saved for climax dohas/chaupais).

REFRAIN MECHANICS:
   - [Chorus] sthayi appears ONCE at top of chapter (3 lines).
   - NO mid-chapter or end-chapter chorus repeat. Total Devanagari
     content lines per chapter = 31 exactly (3 sthayi + 7 x 4 chaupais).

------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 3 - STRICT FACT VALIDATION
------------------------------------------------------------
Cross-check each chaupai against Tulsidas's Ramcharitmanas. Categorize
findings:

   HARD FACTUAL ERROR   - directly contradicts canonical Tulsidas
   MAJOR OMISSION       - signature Tulsidas episode entirely absent
   MINOR DEPARTURE      - small detail varied / oversimplified

Red-flag scan checklist:
   - Yuga errors (Ram is Treta, NOT Kaliyug)
   - Cross-avatar conflation (Ram's bal-leela is NOT Krishna's
     butter-stealing)
   - Forced rhyme padding (no coined words like ललाम, धारि)
   - Wrong word-sense (e.g., अनंग = Kamadeva, NOT "humble")
   - Loose final-consonant rhymes (Fix D violation: -ार with mixed
     preceding syllables is INVALID; must be full-tail match)
   - Sumitra-from-both-queens must be present in any Balkand chapter
     covering kheer-vitaran
   - Tulsidas-specific avatar-katha (Narad-moh, Manu-Shatarupa,
     Pratapbhanu) MUST appear in any opening Balkand chapter about
     avatar-cause
   - Ahalya rejoins Gautama (per Tulsidas; not solo tap)
   - Valmiki-style "stri-vadh dilemma" at Tadaka-vadh is absent in
     Tulsidas; use muni-aagya frame

------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 4 - VINTAGE LYRICIST / COMPOSER POLISH
------------------------------------------------------------
SINGABILITY (composer's lens):
   - No Sanskrit conjunct stacks at line climax.
   - End every line on an open long vowel.
   - Keep matra count consistent (~14-16) within a chaupai.
   - All chaupais STRICT AABB. No AAAA chaupais. No AABB-AABB
     drifting into AAAA (must be 2 distinct rhyme-pairs per chaupai).
   - No ज़ (nukta) -- use देशी ज.
   - Soft consonants preferred at line-ends.

LYRIC CRAFT (lyricist's lens):
   - No filler-padding words.
   - Concrete imagery preferred over abstract noun-stacks.
   - Stock words capped (see TC-L-004 in Test_Cases.txt for per-chapter
     caps).
   - One vivid adjective beats three generic ones.
   - Refrain power: sthayi is verbatim Tulsidas, so this is built-in.

AWADHI BALANCE:
   - Aim for 2-4 distinct Awadhi participles per chapter (cumulative
     across the 7 chaupais).
   - Don't go full Awadhi (vocabulary must remain accessible to a
     modern Hindi listener).

------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 5 - SUNO FORMATTING
------------------------------------------------------------
Convert polished lyrics into Suno-ready text:

1. STRIP all of:
   - File-level title headers
   - English transliterations and translations
   - Raag-note lines
   - Structural-note blockquotes
   - Chapter title headers
   - Chaupai sub-labels
   - Numeric line prefixes
   - Line-verification footers

2. ADD structure tags (English, in square brackets, on own lines):
   [Chorus]            - on the sthayi block (3 lines)
   [Verse 1] ... [Verse 7]
   [Outro]             - after final verse

3. (Removed in v2.1.) No mid-chapter or end-chapter chorus repeat.
   The 3-line sthayi appears exactly once, at the top of the chapter.

4. ADD per-chapter intensity tags at key arc moments. Place on own
   lines BEFORE the relevant [Verse N] tag. See Section 3.5 for the
   tag glossary.

------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 6 - STYLE PROMPT
------------------------------------------------------------
Strictly under 1000 characters (Suno 5.5 style-box limit).

Mandatory voice descriptor (paste verbatim in every chapter):

   "warm soulful male baritone, slightly nasal resonance,
    plaintive heart-tugging timbre, restrained vibrato,
    mid-to-low range, earnest sincere delivery, smooth legato,
    vintage Hindi film bhajan playback style, kathavachak
    storyteller tone"

NEVER name singers, composers, or labels.

Per-chapter modulation:
   - Anchor raag (Section 3.2)
   - Tempo (vilambit / medium kaharwa / etc.)
   - Rasa adjective(s)
   - Mood arc through verses
   - Distinct lead instrument(s)
   - Vocal modulation note
   - Production note

------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 7 - FILE OUTPUT
------------------------------------------------------------
File naming pattern:
   <KaandName>_Ch<N>_<EpisodeTitleInEnglish>.txt

Folder: Tulsi\<KaandName>\

Final folder state: typically 6-10 .txt files per kaand folder
(depending on episode density). No .md versions, no separate
style-prompt file, no leftovers.

3.4 RHYME VOCABULARY - AWADHI/TULSIDAS-STYLE WORKING SETS
------------------------------------------------------------
For AABB chaupais, the FINAL 2 SYLLABLES must match within each pair.
The following families work well at pada-ends:

-ाए (Awadhi/Hindi past-perfect, soft):
   पाए, भाए, सुहाए, बढ़ाए, गाए, जगाए, बहाए, गराए
-ाई (Awadhi causative feminine):
   सुनाई, बढ़ाई, सुहाई, पाई, माई, बताई, रचाई, सजाई,
   प्रकटाई, समझाई, गहाई, बहाई
-ीन्हा (Awadhi past-perfect):
   कीन्हा, दीन्हा, लीन्हा (= लिया), मीन्हा (less common)
-आयो (Awadhi simple past):
   आयो, गयो, छायो, जायो, समायो, बँचायो, समावायो
-भयो / -भा (Awadhi 'became'):
   भयो, भा (line-end), उपजा-भा
-ामा (vocative / declarative Awadhi):
   धामा, नामा, सामा, समा, श्यामा, गामा, कामा (-ाम-ा)
-ीरा (masc.-sing. Awadhi adjective):
   वीरा, पीरा, धीरा, गहीरा, अधीरा, मीरा, फकीरा
-ंगा / -ंगी (Awadhi -ng family):
   संगा, तरंगा, अंगा, प्रसंगा, रंगा, अनंगा, अभंगा
-ारी (Awadhi feminine; works for Mangal-Bhavan-style sthayis):
   हारी, बिहारी, सारी, प्यारी, न्यारी, बारी, झारी,
   बखारी, उजियारी
-ोका (Awadhi -oka tail):
   सोका, लोका, त्रैलोका, धोका, मोका, अशोका
-ाथा (-ATHA tail):
   गाथा, नाथा, माथा, साथा
-ागर (sthayi-style for Hanuman couplet):
   सागर, उजागर, नागर, गागर
-एत (preserved):
   हेत, निकेत, समेत, खेत, चेत
-ाँव (preserved, careful pool):
   गाँव, छाँव, ठाँव, नाँव, पाँव

AVOID rhymes where the syllable BEFORE the rhyme-syllable varies
across the pair (e.g., 'संचार / व्यवहार' technically both end -ार
but the tails 'चार / हार' differ -- this is a loose rhyme, Fix D
violation).

3.6 PER-KAAND STHAYI COUPLETS (NEW v2)
------------------------------------------------------------
Each kaand uses ONE Tulsidas-signature couplet as the recurring sthayi
across all its chapters. The 3rd line is the universal jay-ghosh
refrain. Lines 1-2 must AABB-rhyme internally (full-syllable tail).

BAL KAND
   मंगल भवन अमंगल हारी।
   द्रवहु सुदसरथ अजिर बिहारी।
   सिय राम जय राम जय श्री राम॥
   (Rhyme L1-L2: -ारी ✓  -- Tulsidas verbatim from Bal Kand)

AYODHYA KAND
   रघुकुल रीति सदा चलि आई।
   प्रान जाई बरु बचन न जाई।
   सिय राम जय राम जय श्री राम॥
   (Rhyme L1-L2: -आई ✓  -- Tulsidas verbatim from Ayodhya Kand)

ARANYA KAND
   वन-वासी राम-सिय-लखन-समाना।
   ऋषि-मुनि-संग सहज-सुख-स्थाना।
   सिय राम जय राम जय श्री राम॥
   (Rhyme L1-L2: -ाना ✓  -- composed in Tulsidas style)

KISHKINDHA KAND
   हनुमत-सुग्रीव-सहायक-संगा।
   राम-कथा शुभ-यात्रा-तरंगा।
   सिय राम जय राम जय श्री राम॥
   (Rhyme L1-L2: -ंगा ✓  -- composed in Tulsidas style)

SUNDAR KAND
   जय हनुमान ज्ञान गुन सागर।
   जय कपीस तिहुं लोक उजागर।
   सिय राम जय राम जय श्री राम॥
   (Rhyme L1-L2: -ागर ✓  -- Tulsidas Hanuman Chalisa verbatim)

LANKA KAND
   रावण-कुल-संहारक-नाथा।

SECTION 4 - ANTI-PATTERNS (v2)
================================================================================

X "कलियुग में" referring to Ram-avatar -- it's Treta Yug.

X Krishna's butter-stealing attributed to Ram's bal-leela.

X AAAA chaupai (all 4 padas on one rhyme).
   -> Tulsidas's pattern is AABB (2 distinct rhyme-pairs per chaupai).

X Loose final-consonant rhymes -- e.g., संचार + व्यवहार + सत्कार.
   These all end -ार but the preceding syllable varies (चार / हार /
   कार). Tulsidas requires FULL 2-syllable tail match.

X Generic mukhda-refrain unrelated to Tulsidas.
   -> Use one of the seven Tulsidas-signature couplets (Section 3.6).

X No Awadhi participles at all.
   -> Sprinkle 2-4 per chapter (कीन्हा, दीन्हा, आयो, गयो, भयो,
      पाई, सुहाई etc.).

X Long-line (~24 matra) bhajan-style padas.
   -> Tulsidas chaupai padas are ~14-16 matras each. Tighten.

X Forced/coined rhyme words.

X Sumitra's TWO sons unexplained (must say she received from both
  Kaushalya AND Kaikeyi).

X Tulsidas avatar-katha skipped (Narad-moh, Manu-Shatarupa,
  Pratapbhanu must appear in opening Bal Kand chapters).

X Astra-stacking compounds.

X Heavy Sanskrit conjunct at line-end.

X ज़ (nukta) in devotional verse.

X Artist names in style prompt.

X Abstract imagery without concrete grounding.

X Ahalya solo-tap after redemption (per Tulsidas, she rejoins Gautama).

X Valmiki-style stri-vadh dilemma at Tadaka-vadh.

X Number lines 1-7 chaupais beyond 7 in a single chapter.
  -> If content exceeds 7 chaupais, SPLIT into an additional chapter.
