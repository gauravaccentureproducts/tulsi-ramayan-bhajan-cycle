SECTION 1 - PROJECT FOUNDATIONS
================================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------
1.1 VISION
------------------------------------------------------------
Compose a complete devotional bhajan cycle covering ALL SEVEN kaands of
Goswami Tulsidas's Shri Ramcharitmanas, rendered in the kathavachak
(storyteller) musical style of the vintage Hindi film bhajan tradition,
for modern devotional listening. The cycle reads as a seamless retelling,
faithful to Tulsidas's narrative sequence and written in accessible Hindi.

------------------------------------------------------------
1.2 SCOPE - SEVEN KAANDS
------------------------------------------------------------
   1. Bal Kand        (बाल काण्ड)        - childhood, marriage         [DONE]
   2. Ayodhya Kand    (अयोध्या काण्ड)     - exile, vanvas
   3. Aranya Kand     (अरण्य काण्ड)       - forest, Sita-haran
   4. Kishkindha Kand (किष्किन्धा काण्ड)   - alliance with Sugriva
   5. Sundar Kand     (सुन्दर काण्ड)       - Hanuman's Lanka journey
   6. Lanka Kand      (लंका काण्ड)         - the war
   7. Uttar Kand      (उत्तर काण्ड)        - return, Ram-rajya

Recommended order: Balkand -> Ayodhya -> Aranya -> Kishkindha -> Sundar
-> Lanka -> Uttar.

------------------------------------------------------------
1.3 FIXED PER-KAAND STRUCTURE
------------------------------------------------------------
Total per kaand           : 255 lines (exact)
Chapters per kaand        : 5 (exact)
Lines per chapter         : 51 (exact)

Per-chapter anatomy:
   - Mangal / Mukhda      : 3 lines (refrain; returns between antaras)
   - 12 chaupais          : 12 x 4 lines = 48 lines
   - Chapter total        : 3 + 48 = 51 lines

Grand total across full cycle: 255 x 7 = 1,785 lines

SECTION 2 - THE 7-PHASE WORKFLOW
================================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 1 - PLAN
------------------------------------------------------------
1. Pick the kaand to compose.
2. Look up its canonical Tulsidas episode list (Section 3.1).
3. Distribute the episodes across 5 chapters (51 lines each). Rough
   rule: one chapter = one self-contained narrative arc.
4. Pick the anchor raag for this kaand (Section 3.2).
5. Decide rasa modulation per chapter (Section 3.3).

Example allocation (Balkand):
   Ch 1 - Invocation + Avatar Cause (Narad-moh, Manu-Shatarupa,
           Pratapbhanu, Prithvi-Vishnu pledge, Ayodhya setup)
   Ch 2 - Yajna, Ram-janma, four brothers, bal-leela, gurukul
   Ch 3 - Vishwamitra's arrival, Tadaka-vadh, Maricha-Subahu,
           yajna-raksha
   Ch 4 - Ahalya-uddhar, Mithila, Sita's first darshan
   Ch 5 - Dhanush-bhang, Parashuram-samvad, chaturvivah

------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 2 - DRAFT LYRICS
------------------------------------------------------------
For each of 5 chapters, write 51 lines:

   - Mukhda (3 lines): includes a recurring jay-ghosh phrase
     ("श्री राम जय राम जय...") on line 3 to bind chapters.
   - 12 chaupais (4 lines each): one episode per chaupai, in
     canonical Tulsidas sequence.

Rules:
   - Simple Hindi. Accessible to a Hindi listener without glossary.
     No heavy Sanskrit padding.
   - AAAA rhyme dominant per chaupai (all 4 lines rhyme on the same
     ending sound). Long-vowel endings preferred. See Section 3.4
     for the working vocabulary.
   - Number lines 1-51 within the chapter during drafting (later
     stripped for Suno output).
   - Devanagari script only in verse body. English permitted only
     later in [tags].
   - Refrain doxology: "श्री राम जय राम जय..." or context-appropriate
     jay-ghosh recurs as the binding sthayee.
   - Tone: reverent, kathavachak, narrative. No colloquialisms that
     break bhakti mood.

------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 3 - STRICT FACT VALIDATION
------------------------------------------------------------
Cross-check each chaupai against Tulsidas's Ramcharitmanas. Categorize
findings:

   HARD FACTUAL ERROR   - directly contradicts canonical Tulsidas
   MAJOR OMISSION       - signature Tulsidas episode entirely absent
   MINOR DEPARTURE      - small detail varied / oversimplified

Red-flag scan checklist:
   - Yuga errors (Ram is Treta, NOT Kaliyug)
   - Cross-avatar conflation (Ram's bal-leela is NOT Krishna's butter
     stealing; Ram's diction is dignified, not Krishna's mischief)
   - Forced rhyme padding (any word that exists only for rhyme,
     not meaning - e.g., ललाम, हुई हृदय की पाँव, धारि as filler)
   - Wrong word-sense (e.g., अनंग = Kamadeva, NOT "humble")
   - Sumitra-from-both-queens missing (the Tulsidas signature detail:
     she receives kheer portions from BOTH Kaushalya AND Kaikeyi,
     hence two sons)
   - Tulsidas-specific avatar-katha missing (Narad-moh, Manu-Shatarupa,
     Pratapbhanu) - these MUST appear in any opening kaand chapter
     about avatar-cause; otherwise the bhajan reads like Valmiki
   - Ahalya solo-tap after redemption (per Tulsidas she rejoins Gautama;
     don't end her arc with isolation)
   - Valmiki-style "stri-vadh dilemma" at Tadaka-vadh (Tulsidas has
     Ram simply follow muni-aagya)

Fix hard errors immediately. Then add any missing major episodes by
inserting new chaupais OR compressing existing ones to make room.

------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 4 - VINTAGE LYRICIST / COMPOSER POLISH
------------------------------------------------------------
Run a singability and craft pass. From the perspective of a vintage
music director and lyricist:

SINGABILITY (composer's lens):
   - No Sanskrit conjunct clusters at line climax. Stacks like
     "ब्रह्मास्त्र-वरुणास्त्र-अग्न्यास्त्र" are unsingable.
   - End every line on an open long vowel - sustainable on the
     raag's vadi/samvadi (e.g., Yaman's Ga and Ni).
   - Keep meter consistent within a chaupai - each of the 4 lines
     should have ~14-16 matras.
   - Convert any AABB chaupai to AAAA. One rhyme rule per song.
   - No ज़ (nukta) - breaks devotional register; use देशी ज.
   - Soft consonants flow better at line-ends than hard stops.

LYRIC CRAFT (lyricist's lens):
   - Replace filler-padding words. If a word exists only for rhyme
     and adds no meaning, rewrite the line.
   - Prefer CONCRETE image over abstract noun. Replace 'मन में आशा-धूप'
     (hope-sunshine?) with a specific image (lamp, river, ankle-bell).
   - Diversify line-ends: stock words महान / अभिराम / जय-जयकार /
     धाम / नाम / गान appear too often if not policed; reserve them
     for true climaxes.
   - One vivid adjective beats three generic ones (avoid
     'सुन्दर मधुर महान' pattern).
   - Maintain register consistency - do not jump between rural folk-
     Hindi and tatsama Sanskrit within the same chaupai.
   - Refrain power: mukhda must hook the listener. Consider a
     recurring binding phrase across chapter mukhdas.

------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 5 - SUNO FORMATTING
------------------------------------------------------------
Convert polished lyrics into Suno-ready text:

1. STRIP all of:
   - File-level title headers
   - English transliterations and translations
   - Raag-note lines
   - Structural-note blockquotes
   - Chapter title headers
   - Chaupai sub-labels (e.g., '### चौपाई १ — गणेश-शारदा वंदना')
   - Numeric line prefixes (1., 2., etc.)
   - Line-verification footers
   - Single-quote marks around dialogue (keep em-dashes - Suno
     parses these as voice-change cues)

2. ADD structure tags (English, in square brackets, on own lines):
   [Chorus]            - on the mukhda block
   [Verse 1] ... [Verse 12]   - for the 12 chaupais
   [Outro]             - after Verse 12

3. ADD chorus returns (re-paste the chorus lyrics, not just the tag):
   - Before [Verse 5]
   - Before [Verse 9]
   - For the FINAL chapter of the kaand: also after [Verse 12],
     before [Outro], for grand-finale effect.

4. ADD per-chapter intensity tags at key arc moments. Place on own
   lines BEFORE the relevant [Verse N] tag. See Section 3.5 for the
   tag glossary.

------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 6 - STYLE PROMPT
------------------------------------------------------------
Write ONE style prompt per chapter. Strictly under 1000 characters
(Suno 5.5 style-box limit).

Mandatory voice description (consistent across ALL chapters of ALL
kaands - paste verbatim):

   "warm soulful male baritone, slightly nasal resonance,
    plaintive heart-tugging timbre, restrained vibrato,
    mid-to-low range, earnest sincere delivery, smooth legato,
    vintage Hindi film bhajan playback style, kathavachak
    storyteller tone"

This describes the desired vintage devotional voice WITHOUT naming
any artist. NEVER name singers or composers in the prompt.

Per-chapter modulation (vary based on rasa):
   - Anchor raag (from Section 3.2)
   - Tempo (vilambit / medium kaharwa / medium-brisk / dramatic
     build)
   - Rasa adjective(s) - peaceful contemplative / joyful celebration /
     vir-rasa heroic / karuna softening into shringar / dramatic
     peak then utsav
   - Mood arc through the verses (specific to chapter content)
   - Distinct lead instrument(s) (bansuri / shehnai / sarangi /
     pakhawaj / nagara-dhol / sitar / conch / harmonium / tanpura)
   - Vocal modulation note (declamatory / tender / full-throated /
     contemplative)
   - Production note (warm acoustic, natural reverb, vintage
     devotional aesthetic, no modern electronics)

Structure as ONE flowing paragraph. Verify char-count under 1000.
Trim by cutting:
   - Redundant adjective pairs
   - Sandhi-prakash and other technical raag terminology
   - Verbose production descriptions

------------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 7 - FILE OUTPUT
------------------------------------------------------------
Save each chapter as a single .txt file in Tulsi\<KaandName>\, with
both blocks separated by clear markers:

   ========== STYLE (paste into Suno style box) ==========

   [style prompt text]


   ========== LYRICS (paste into Suno lyrics box) ==========

   [Vilambit alaap]      (chapter-1 opener tag if applicable)
   [Chorus]
   ... 3 mukhda lines ...

   [Verse 1]
   ... 4 lines ...

   ... etc. through Verse 12, with chorus returns at V5 and V9 ...

   [Outro]


File naming pattern:
   <KaandName>_Ch<N>_<EpisodeTitleInEnglish>.txt

Example (Balkand):
   Balkand_Ch1_Invocation_and_Avatar_Cause.txt
   Balkand_Ch2_Yajna_Birth_and_Pastimes.txt
   ...

Final folder state: exactly 5 .txt files per kaand folder.
No .md versions, no separate style-prompt file, no leftovers.

------------------------------------------------------------
3.1 CANONICAL TULSIDAS EPISODES PER KAAND
------------------------------------------------------------
Narrative sequence MUST follow Ramcharitmanas chapter-by-chapter.
No invented episodes. No skipping canonical events.

BAL KAND (255 lines / 5 chapters)
   - Mangalacharan (Ganesh, Saraswati, Shiva, Hanuman, Guru,
     Tulsidas vandana)
   - AVATAR-KATHA (Tulsidas-specific - DO NOT SKIP):
        * Narad-moh: Narad's pride; Vishnu's monkey-face leela;
          curse becomes Sita-viyog + vanara-sahay foreshadowing
        * Manu-Shatarupa: tapasya; Vishnu's pledge to be born as
          their son (they become Dasharath and Kaushalya)
        * Pratapbhanu: virtuous king tricked by deceitful sage;
          reborn as Ravana, Kumbhakaran, and Vibhishan trio
   - Ravana's tyranny in Treta (NOT Kaliyug); Brahma boon
   - Prithvi takes cow form, plea to Brahma; all reach Kshirsagar
   - Vishnu's pledge: four forms in Dasharath's house
   - Ayodhya setup; three queens; childless king's grief
   - Vashishtha advises Putrakameshti yajna; Shringi rishi invited
   - Yajna; yajna-purusha appears with kheer
   - Kheer distribution: 1/2 Kaushalya, 1/4 Kaikeyi, BOTH give
     Sumitra portions of theirs - hence Sumitra has TWO sons
   - Birth of four princes - Chaitra Shukla Navami; Ram in
     4-armed form then becomes baby
   - Naming by Vashishtha: Ram, Bharat, Lakshman, Shatrughna
   - Bal-leela - dust-coated brothers' play, NOT butter-stealing
     (that is Krishna)
   - Gurukul-shiksha with Vashishtha
   - Vishwamitra's arrival; Dasharath's distress; Vashishtha
     consoles
   - Vana-yatra; Bala-Atibala mantras
   - Tadaka-vadh - Ram follows muni-aagya (NOT Valmiki-style
     stri-vadh dilemma)
   - Divyastra-pradan; Siddhashram; Maricha thrown far, Subahu
     killed
   - Ahalya-uddhar at Gautam's ashram; she REJOINS Gautama (per
     Tulsidas; not solo tap)
   - Mithila arrival; Janak's welcome
   - Pushp-vatika; Sita comes for Gauri puja; first-darshan moment
   - Sita's prayer at Gauri mandir
   - Dhanush-yajna; kings fail; Janak's lament; Lakshman's anger
   - Vishwamitra commands Ram; Ram lifts and breaks Pinaka
   - Sita's varmala
   - Parashuram's arrival in fury; Lakshman-Parashuram samvad
     (one of the LONGEST Tulsidas sections - allow space)
   - Ram lifts Vishnu's bow (Sharanga); Parashuram recognizes him
   - Chaturvivah - Ram-Sita, Lakshman-Urmila, Bharat-Mandavi,
     Shatrughna-Shrutakirti (latter two are Kushadhwaj's daughters)
   - Baraat returns to Ayodhya

AYODHYA KAND
   - Rajya-tilak preparation - Dasharath decides to coronate Ram
   - Manthara poisons Kaikeyi; reminds her of the two boons
   - Kaikeyi demands: Bharat enthroned, Ram exiled for 14 years
   - Dasharath's anguish; cannot break his word
   - Ram accepts vanvas calmly; Sita and Lakshman insist on
     accompanying
   - Departure from Ayodhya; people follow weeping
   - Guha-milap at Shringverpur (Guha is the nishadraj-boatman
     friend)
   - Ganga-paar; Bharadwaj-ashram
   - Reaching Chitrakoot; ashram-life begins
   - Dasharath's death from grief
   - Bharat returns from Kekaya; refuses the throne
   - Bharat-milap at Chitrakoot; Bharat asks Ram to return
   - Ram refuses (dharma binds him); gives padukas to Bharat
   - Bharat returns to Ayodhya; places padukas on throne;
     lives ascetically at Nandigram

ARANYA KAND
   - Atri Muni and Anasuya's ashram; Anasuya gives Sita divine
     ornaments and pati-dharma upadesh
   - Sharabhanga muni; gives Ram his asceticism's punya, enters fire
   - Sutikshna muni; Agastya muni; Agastya gives Ram divine weapons
   - Panchavati - Lakshman builds parnashala (hut)
   - Surpanakha arrives; tries to seduce Ram and Lakshman;
     Lakshman cuts her nose/ears
   - Khar, Dushan, Trishira attack with army; Ram slays all
     (14,000 rakshasas in fourteen muhurtas)
   - Surpanakha goes to Ravana, incites him
   - Ravana plots Sita-haran with Maricha as accomplice
   - Maricha as golden deer (kanchan-mrig); Sita asks Ram to fetch
   - Ram pursues; "Help Lakshman!" call traps Lakshman too
   - Ravana arrives as sannyasi; abducts Sita; Jatayu fights, falls
   - Ram-Lakshman return; find empty ashram; great grief
   - Jatayu's death; Ram performs his last rites
   - Kabandha encounter; he is freed from curse by Ram
   - Shabari's ashram; she has been waiting; offers berries
     (Tulsidas: Shabari giving tasted berries to ensure sweetness;
     Ram accepts with love)
   - Shabari gives directions to Rishyamuka and Sugriva

KISHKINDHA KAND
   - Hanuman-milan; Hanuman in brahmin guise meets Ram-Lakshman
   - Hanuman recognizes the divine; carries them to Sugriva
   - Sugriva-mitrata (agni-sakshi - friendship sealed by fire)
   - Sugriva's story; Vali's enmity
   - Vali-vadh - Ram slays Vali from concealment (Tulsidas handles
     with explanations)
   - Tara's lament; Vali's wisdom; Angad's status
   - Sugriva enthroned; he forgets Ram during monsoon
   - Lakshman's anger; Sugriva remembers duty
   - Vanara-senas dispatched in four directions
   - Southern party (Hanuman, Angad, Jambavan, Nala, Nila) reaches
     sea
   - Sampati (Jatayu's brother) confirms Sita is in Lanka
   - Hanuman alone can leap the ocean
   - Kand ends with Hanuman about to leap

SUNDAR KAND
   - Hanuman's leap across the ocean
   - Surasa - testing Hanuman; he passes by cleverness
   - Simhika - rakshasi devours shadows; Hanuman kills her
   - Entry into Lanka in tiny form; explores by night
   - Vibhishan's house - they meet, Vibhishan tells Sita's location
   - Ashok-vatika - Sita captive under trees, surrounded by
     rakshasis
   - Ravana arrives, threatens Sita; she refuses; Trijata's dream
   - Hanuman drops Ram's mudrika; Sita gives chudamani for Ram
   - Ashok-vatika destroyed; Hanuman caught
   - Brought before Ravana; Hanuman's tail set afire
   - Lanka-dahan - Hanuman burns Lanka with his tail
   - Returns to Sita; she blesses him
   - Crosses back; meets vanara army; informs Ram
   - Vibhishan-sharanagati (per Tulsidas, this happens at END of
     Sundar Kand, NOT in Lanka Kand)
   - Setu-bandh prarambh

LANKA KAND (Yuddha Kand)
   - Setu-bandh completion - vanaras build the bridge
   - Ram-Lakshman cross with army
   - Angad-dautya - Angad as messenger to Ravana
   - Ravana spurns peace
   - War begins; multiple days of battle
   - Kumbhakaran-vadh - Ram slays Kumbhakaran
   - Meghnad's battles; Lakshman struck by shakti
   - Sushen-vaidya; Hanuman fetches Sanjivani booti
   - Lakshman revived
   - Meghnad-vadh by Lakshman
   - Ravana-Ram final battle
   - Ravana-vadh
   - Vibhishan crowned king of Lanka
   - Sita-agni-pariksha (Tulsidas treats this delicately)
   - Sita-Ram reunion
   - Pushpak-viman; return to Ayodhya
   - Bharat-milap; coronation imminent

UTTAR KAND
   - Ayodhya-return; the long-awaited reunion
   - Ram-rajya-abhishek - the coronation
   - Ram-rajya described - utopia of dharma
   - Kakbhushundi-Garuda samvad - the major discourse of Uttar Kand;
     story of Kakbhushundi who tells Garuda about Ram-bhakti
   - Concluding bhakti-mahatmya
   - Phal-shruti - the fruits of hearing/reciting Ramayan

------------------------------------------------------------
3.2 ANCHOR RAAG PER KAAND
------------------------------------------------------------
Yaman anchors the cycle; per-kaand pairings honor each kaand's rasa.

Bal Kand        - YAMAN (mangal, innocence, devotion);
                  close with Bhairavi for aarti-feel
Ayodhya Kand    - BHAIRAV / PURIYA DHANASHRI (viraha, separation,
                  lament)
Aranya Kand     - MALKAUNS / BHOPALI (forest depth, ascetic bhakti)
Kishkindha Kand - PAHADI / BRINDAVANI SARANG (friendship, daytime)
Sundar Kand     - HAMIR / BHAIRAVI (Hanuman's valor + bhakti)
Lanka Kand      - MARWA / ADANA / SHREE (vir rasa, intensity, war)
Uttar Kand      - YAMAN / BHUPALI (shanti, full circle)

Raag Yaman quick-reference (anchor raag):
   - Thaat   : Kalyan
   - Time    : evening (sandhi-prakash, ~6-9 PM)
   - Swaras  : all shuddha except Madhyam (tivra)
   - Vadi    : Gandhar (Ga)
   - Samvadi : Nishad (Ni)
   - Rasa    : peaceful, devotional, serene; bhakti + shringar

Laya / tempo:
   - Mukhda     : vilambit (slow), alaap-like
   - Chaupais   : madhya laya - Kaharwa (8-beat) or Dadra (6-beat)

------------------------------------------------------------
3.3 RASA MODULATION PER CHAPTER (TYPICAL ARC)
------------------------------------------------------------
A kaand spans an emotional arc. Allocate chapters along that arc.

Example (Balkand):
   Ch 1 - shanta / bhakti      (invocation + cosmic backdrop)
   Ch 2 - mangal-utsav         (yajna, birth, bal-leela)
   Ch 3 - vir-rasa             (Vishwamitra, demon slaying)
   Ch 4 - karuna -> shringar   (Ahalya, first-darshan)
   Ch 5 - dramatic peak + utsav (dhanush-bhang, weddings)

Each chapter's style prompt should call out:
   - The dominant rasa
   - The arc within the chapter (build / peak / settling)
   - Specific lead instruments matching the rasa

-ात : साथ, बात, सौगात, दिन-रात, गात
-ाज : महाराज, सिंहासन-साज, ब्रह्म-विराज, काज, समाज, आवाज, आज,
       राज, सरताज
-ोर : घोर, शोर, पुरजोर, कोलाहल-तोर, चहुँओर, जोर, छोर, विभोर
-एत : हेत, निकेत, खेत, समेत
-ावे : कहावे, बतावे, सजावे, बजावे, गिरावे
-मय : विस्मय, अमय, रसमय, निर्मय, आनंदमय
-ाँव : छाँव, ठाँव, गाँव, नाँव (small word-pool; use carefully)

Avoid rhyme-families that lock in awkward words.
Avoid coining non-existent words just to satisfy a rhyme.

SECTION 4 - ANTI-PATTERNS (LESSONS FROM BALKAND)
================================================================================

X "कलियुग में बढ़ने लगा... रावण..."
   - Ram-avatar is Treta Yug. Use "त्रेता में".

X "मक्खन-माखन लूटते" attributed to Ram
   - That is Krishna's Vrindavan leela.
   - Ram's bal-leela: dust-coated brothers' play, crawling,
     falling, being held by mother, toy bow-arrows, anklet-bells
     (पगिया).

X "कौसल्या सुलक्षणा"
   - Reads as a queen named "Sulakshana" (does not exist).
   - Use clearer grammar; "Kaushalya, of auspicious marks" if
     needed, or drop the word.

X Forced rhyme words (kill with fire):
   - ललाम (was filler; removed)
   - हुई हृदय की पाँव (meaningless)
   - शाप-दुख-धारि (coined "धारि")
   - दुष्ट-कमान (Tadaka had no bow)
   - विवाह-गान (coined compound)
   - अनंग used to mean "humble" (अनंग = Kamadeva)

X Sumitra's TWO sons unexplained
   - Canonical Tulsidas detail: Sumitra received kheer from BOTH
     Kaushalya AND Kaikeyi.
   - State this explicitly or you miss the famous moment.

X Avatar-katha skipped
   - Tulsidas's Balkand has Narad-moh, Manu-Shatarupa, Pratapbhanu
     BEFORE Ravana's tyranny.
   - Skipping these reduces the bhajan to a Valmiki-style summary,
     not Tulsidas.

X Astra-stacking compounds ("ब्रह्मास्त्र-वरुणास्त्र-अग्न्यास्त्र")
   - Unsingable.
   - Either single weapon or generic "दिव्य-शस्त्र".

X Heavy Sanskrit conjunct at line-end
   - Kills sustained note on raag.
   - Use open-vowel endings.

X ज़ (nukta) in devotional verse
   - Breaks register. Use देशी ज.

X AABB rhyme inside chaupai
   - Composer wants AAAA per chaupai.
   - Convert any mixed-rhyme chaupai to single-rhyme.

X Artist names in style prompt
   - NEVER name singers, composers, or labels.
   - Describe vocal characteristics instead.

X Abstract imagery ("मन में आशा-धूप", "जागे प्रभु अनुराग")
   - Replace with concrete physical images.

X Stock-word repetition
   - महान, अभिराम, जय-जयकार, धाम, नाम, गान should be reserved
     for climaxes, not appear every other chaupai.

X Ahalya stays in solo tap after redemption
   - Per Tulsidas, she REJOINS Gautama (पतिहि मिलि अनुग्रह बखानी).
   - Do not end her arc with isolation.

X Valmiki-style "स्त्री-वध dilemma" at Tadaka-vadh
   - Tulsidas has Ram simply follow muni-aagya.
   - Skip the moral dilemma framing; use muni-aagya frame.

SECTION 7 - WORKED EXAMPLE: HOW BALKAND CH 1 WAS CRAFTED
================================================================================
A concrete walk-through of the 7-phase workflow applied to
Balkand Chapter 1.

PHASE 1 - PLAN:
   Episodes to cover: Mangalacharan + Tulsidas avatar-katha +
   Ravana's tyranny + Vishnu's pledge + Ayodhya setup.
   Anchor raag: Yaman. Rasa: shanta / bhakti / cosmic.
   Allocate 12 chaupais:
      1-3:  Vandanas (Ganesh-Sharada, Shiv-Hanumat, Guru-Tulsidas)
      4-6:  Avatar-katha (Narad-moh, Manu-Shatarupa, Pratapbhanu)
      7:    Treta tyranny / Ravana
      8:    Prithvi-pukar + Vishnu-pledge
      9:    Ayodhya + putraheen
      10:   Vashishtha's Putrakameshti advice
      11:   Yajna preparation anticipation
      12:   Samapan / transition to Ch 2

PHASE 2 - DRAFT MUKHDA:
   Goal: 3 lines, AAAA rhyme, jay-ghosh refrain on line 3.
   Initial draft:
      "राम कथा मंगलमयी, सुनो लगा कर ध्यान।
       तुलसी रचित मानस का, गाऊँ बाल-कथा-गान।
       श्री राम जय राम जय, बोलो जय श्री राम॥"
   Note: "लगा कर ध्यान" has filler; "बाल-कथा-गान" is a triple
   compound.
   After Phase 4 polish:
      "राम कथा मंगलमयी, सुनो धर मन ध्यान।
       तुलसी रचित मानस का, गाऊँ बालकाण्ड-गान।
       श्री राम जय राम जय, बोलो जय श्री राम॥"

PHASE 2 - DRAFT CHAUPAIS:
   Each chaupai = 4 lines, AAAA, long-vowel ending.
   Example for chaupai 4 (Narad-moh):
      "नारद-मुनि अभिमान भरे, गए विष्णु के पास।
       हरि-रूप माँगा प्रभु से, मिला कपि-मुख खास।
       श्राप दिया मुनि ने प्रभु को - सहो विरह की प्यास।
       वानर बनें सहायक तुम्हारे - श्राप फला अति-त्रास॥"
   End-words: पास, खास, प्यास, त्रास - all -ास, AAAA, long vowel.
   "हरि-रूप" carries the classic double-meaning (Vishnu OR monkey)
   that Tulsidas plays on.

PHASE 3 - FACT VALIDATION:
   - Spotted "कलियुग में" in original Ch 1 chaupai 4 - HARD ERROR.
     Ram is Treta Yug. Fixed to "त्रेता में".
   - Spotted absence of Narad-moh, Manu-Shatarupa, Pratapbhanu in
     first draft - MAJOR OMISSIONS. Added them as new chaupais
     4-6. Compressed old generic content into chaupais 7-9 to
     make room.
   - "सुलक्षणा" reading as a non-existent queen name - MINOR
     DEPARTURE. Rewrote chaupai 9 to remove.

PHASE 4 - VINTAGE POLISH:
   - Mukhda line 1: "सुनो लगा कर ध्यान" -> "सुनो धर मन ध्यान"
     (removes filler).
   - Mukhda line 2: "बाल-कथा-गान" -> "बालकाण्ड-गान"
     (unstack compound).
   - Chaupai 1 (Ganesh-Sharada): converted AABB (आप, संताप,
     वरदान, महान) to AAAA on -ार (विकार, बहार, संचार, धार).
   - Chaupai 2 (Shiv-Hanumat): converted AABB to AAAA on -ाम
     (प्रणाम, राम, याम, प्रभु-काम); added bhajan-idiom
     "आठों याम".
   - Chaupai 4 (Narad): line "मिला कपि-मुख खास" replaces
     awkward "बना मुनि-मुख कपि-विशाल".
   - Chaupai 7: "मचाया महा-संहार" (hard consonant cluster)
     -> "फैले शोक-संहार".
   - Chaupai 8: "देव-संचार" (wrong sense - "circulation of
     gods"?) -> "देव-परिवार" (family of gods).
   - Chaupai 9: "महान-वास" -> "सुख-वास" (simpler, accurate).
   - Chaupai 11: "खुले भाग्य-प्रसंग" (abstract) -> "गूँजे
     शुभ-प्रसंग" (concrete sound resonance).
