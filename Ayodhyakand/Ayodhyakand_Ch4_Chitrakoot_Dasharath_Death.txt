========== STYLE (paste into Suno style box) ==========

Hindi devotional bhajan in Raag Bhairav, serenity broken by tragedy then resolve. Lead vocal: warm soulful male baritone, slightly nasal resonance, plaintive heart-tugging timbre, restrained vibrato expanding into deep lament at Dasharath's death, mid-to-low range, earnest sincere delivery, smooth legato, vintage Hindi film bhajan playback style, kathavachak storyteller tone. Tempo opens peaceful at Chitrakoot ashram, mournful build at Dasharath's grief and death, urgent on Bharat returning from Kekaya. Verses move through Valmiki guiding Ram, Lakshman building parnashala, peaceful ashram-life, Dasharath's lament and death in Ayodhya, Vashishtha summoning Bharat, Bharat returning to find father gone, refusing throne, performing last rites, setting out for Chitrakoot. Instruments: bansuri on Chitrakoot peace, sarangi on death scene, pakhawaj for Bharat's grief, tabla, harmonium pad. Production: warm acoustic, deep reverb on mourning peaks, vintage devotional aesthetic, no electronics.
