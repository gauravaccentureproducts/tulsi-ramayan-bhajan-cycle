========== STYLE (paste into Suno style box) ==========

Hindi devotional bhajan in Raag Malkauns, grief-search-devotion arc. Lead vocal: warm soulful male baritone, slightly nasal resonance, plaintive heart-tugging timbre, restrained vibrato deepening on Ram's grief, mid-to-low range, earnest sincere delivery, smooth legato, vintage Hindi film bhajan playback style, kathavachak storyteller tone. Slow mournful tempo on chorus. Verses move through Ram returning to find Sita gone, dual brothers' search, finding wounded Jatayu, his report of Sita-haran and death, Ram's funeral rites, Kabandha encounter and curse-release, reaching Shabari's ashram, her loving wait, tasted berries offered with devotion, Ram eating with love, Shabari's moksha, directions to Sugriva. Instruments: sarangi on lament, bansuri on Shabari's love, sitar arpeggios, tabla, harmonium pad. Production: warm acoustic, natural reverb, vintage devotional-bhakti aesthetic, no electronics, forest-ashram atmosphere.
