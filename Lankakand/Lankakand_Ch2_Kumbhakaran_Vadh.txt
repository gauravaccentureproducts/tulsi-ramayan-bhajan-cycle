========== STYLE (paste into Suno style box) ==========

Hindi devotional bhajan in Raag Marwa-Adana, vir-rasa giant-slaying drama. Lead vocal: warm soulful male baritone, slightly nasal resonance, plaintive heart-tugging timbre, restrained vibrato expanding into thundering declamation, mid-to-low range opening to broad cinematic delivery, earnest dramatic phrasing, smooth legato, vintage Hindi film bhajan playback style, kathavachak storyteller tone. Building tempo to climax. Verses move through day-2 battle, Ravana waking sleeping Kumbhakaran, giant's hunger feast, entering battlefield like a mountain, devouring vanaras, Hanuman fighting, Ram's arrows cutting arms and head, Kumbhakaran dying with Ram-name, Ravana's grief, Meghnad's vow, Meghnad descends, Lakshman struck by shakti. Instruments: pakhawaj and dhol thundering, conch for giant-arrival, sarangi for grief, tabla, harmonium pad. Production: warm acoustic, epic reverb on giant-fall, vintage devotional-heroic aesthetic, no electronics.
